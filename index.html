Our Story

In the city of Oakhaven, where the rain always seemed to smell like cedar and wet pavement, Elias ran a shop that sold "nearly-dead" clocks. He didn't fix them to be perfect; he fixed them to keep their character. Some ticked with a slight limp, others chimed a note too flat, but Elias loved them for their stubbornness.
